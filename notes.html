INTRODUCTION TO THE CURRICULUM

This 8-week comprehensive personal training curriculum is designed for fitness enthusiasts of all levels. Whether you're a complete beginner or have some experience, this program will guide you through essential training principles, nutrition strategies, and recovery methods.

Curriculum Overview

  • Strength training fundamentals and progressions
  • Cardiovascular health and conditioning
  • Nutrition science and meal planning
  • Recovery optimization and injury prevention
  • Body composition management
  • Flexibility, mobility, and functional movement

How to Use This Program

  • Follow the weekly structure and lessons sequentially
  • Adapt exercises based on your current fitness level
  • Track your progress and adjust as needed
  • Implement dietary recommendations with your current lifestyle
  • Prioritize recovery and listen to your body
  • Consult healthcare professionals before major changes

WEEK 1: FOUNDATION & FITNESS ASSESSMENT

Lesson 1.1: Getting Started - Assessment & Goals

  • Objective: Establish baseline fitness levels and set realistic goals for the 8-week program.
  • Body measurements: Weight, body fat %, chest, waist, hips, arms, thighs
  • Fitness tests: Push-ups, squats, 1.5-mile run/walk, flexibility test
  • Medical clearance: Consult with healthcare provider if new to exercise
  • Goal setting: SMART goals (Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, Time-bound)

Lesson 1.2: Understanding Your Body

  • Objective: Learn basic anatomy relevant to fitness training.
  • Major muscle groups: Chest, back, shoulders, biceps, triceps, forearms
  • Core muscles: Rectus abdominis, obliques, transverse abdominis, erector spinae
  • Lower body: Quadriceps, hamstrings, glutes, calves
  • Joint mechanics: Importance of proper form and range of motion

Lesson 1.3: Nutrition Fundamentals

  • Objective: Understand macronutrients and basic nutrition principles.
  • Macronutrients: Proteins (muscle building), Carbohydrates (energy), Fats (hormone production)
  • Micronutrients: Vitamins and minerals for optimal health
  • Daily caloric needs: Calculate based on activity level
  • Hydration: Importance of water intake for performance and recovery

Lesson 1.4: Introduction to Recovery & Sleep

  • Objective: Establish healthy recovery habits.
  • Sleep importance: 7-9 hours for muscle recovery and hormonal balance
  • Sleep hygiene: Consistent schedule, dark room, cool temperature
  • Active recovery: Light walking, stretching, foam rolling
  • Stress management: Impact of stress on fitness goals

WEEK 2: BUILDING STRENGTH FOUNDATION

Lesson 2.1: Proper Form & Technique

  • Squat: Feet shoulder-width, chest up, knees tracking over toes
  • Deadlift: Neutral spine, hip hinge movement, controlled descent
  • Push-ups: Neutral spine, shoulders engaged, full range of motion
  • Bent-over rows: Neutral spine, pull elbows to ribcage, squeeze shoulder blades
  • Overhead press: Core braced, press straight up, controlled descent

Lesson 2.2: Workout Programming Basics

  • Frequency: 3-4 training days per week for beginners
  • Volume: Sets and reps - Start with 3 sets of 8-12 reps
  • Rest periods: 60-90 seconds between sets for strength training
  • Progressive overload: Gradually increase weight or reps each week
  • Exercise selection: Compound movements (multi-joint) vs isolations

Lesson 2.3: Nutrition for Muscle Building

  • Protein intake: 0.8-1g per pound of body weight daily
  • Carbohydrate timing: Before and after workouts for energy
  • Healthy fats: Nuts, fish, avocado for hormone production
  • Meal frequency: 3-4 balanced meals for steady energy

Lesson 2.4: Understanding Exercise Recovery

  • Muscle protein synthesis: Occurs 24-48 hours post-workout
  • Stretching: 5-10 minutes post-workout to reduce soreness
  • Foam rolling: 1-2 minutes per muscle group for myofascial release
  • When to rest: Listen to your body, avoid overtraining

WEEK 3: CARDIOVASCULAR CONDITIONING

  • Heart rate zones: Resting, moderate intensity, high intensity
  • VO2 max: Maximum oxygen your body can utilize
  • Steady-state cardio: 20-30 mins at moderate intensity
  • Interval training: Alternating high and low intensity bursts
  • HIIT: High-Intensity Interval Training for time efficiency

Balancing Cardio with Strength Training: Frequency: 2-3 cardio sessions per week alongside strength training. Duration: 20-30 minutes to preserve muscle mass. Separate cardio from heavy strength days if possible.

WEEK 4: NUTRITION & DIET FUNDAMENTALS

  • BMR (Basal Metabolic Rate): Calories burned at rest
  • TDEE (Total Daily Energy Expenditure): BMR + activity
  • Caloric deficit: 300-500 calories below TDEE for fat loss
  • Caloric surplus: 300-500 calories above TDEE for muscle gain
  • Protein for muscle gain: 25-35% of total calories (1g per lb)
  • Carbohydrates & Fats: 40-50% Carbs, 20-30% Fats.
  • Supplementation Guide: Whey protein (convenient post-workout), Creatine monohydrate (supports muscle gains), Multivitamin (fills micronutrient gaps), Omega-3 fish oil, Vitamin D3.

WEEK 5: ADVANCED STRENGTH TRAINING

  • Weight progression: Increase by 2.5-5 lbs when completing all reps
  • Rep range progression: Move from 3x10 to 3x12 before increasing weight
  • Upper/Lower split: 2x per week each body part
  • Push/Pull/Legs split: Organize by movement pattern
  • Deload weeks: Reduce volume by 40-50% every 4-6 weeks
  • Drop sets: Reduce weight immediately after reaching failure
  • Supersets: Perform two exercises back-to-back with no rest
  • Tempo training: Control speed (3 sec down, 1 sec up)

WEEK 6: BODY COMPOSITION & FAT LOSS

  • Moderate deficit: 300-500 calorie deficit for sustainable loss
  • Preserve protein: Maintain 1g per lb to preserve muscle
  • Strength training: Essential to signal body to keep muscle
  • Rate of loss: Aim for 0.5-1 lb per week for body composition
  • Whole foods: Prioritize unprocessed options for satiety
  • Volume eating: Low-calorie foods that fill stomach

WEEK 7: RECOVERY, MOBILITY & FLEXIBILITY

  • Sleep duration: 7-9 hours nightly for muscle recovery
  • Foam Rolling & Self-Myofascial Release: Quadriceps, IT band, Hamstrings, Calves, Upper back. 1-2 minutes per area.
  • Dynamic stretching: Before workouts (leg swings, arm circles)
  • Static stretching: After workouts, hold 20-30 seconds each
  • RICE: Rest, Ice, Compression, Elevation for acute injuries

WEEK 8: INTEGRATION & ADVANCED TECHNIQUES

  • Balanced approach: Combine strength, cardio, flexibility, nutrition
  • Sustainability: Choose approaches you can maintain long-term
  • Autoregulation: Adjust based on daily performance and recovery
  • 1-rep max: Test maximum weight for single repetition
  • Fitness tests: Push-ups, pull-ups, 1.5-mile run, vertical jump
  • Variety: Prevent boredom by changing exercises, training styles

Post-8-Week Recommendations

  • Continue 3-4 training days per week of resistance training
  • Include 2-3 moderate cardio sessions weekly
  • Maintain balanced nutrition with 1g protein per pound body weight
  • Prioritize 7-9 hours sleep and active recovery
  • Set new goals and implement next training phase
